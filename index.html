What Are Timbits? A Quick Look at Canada's Favorite Snack

Timbits are Tim Hortons' signature bite-sized donut holes — soft, sweet, perfectly round, and wildly addictive. First introduced in 1976, they were originally made from the dough cut out of the center of full-sized donuts. Today, they're one of the best-selling individual food items in Canadian food service history, with over 2 billion Timbits sold every year across Canada and the United States.

They're made from the same premium yeast or cake donut dough as Tim Hortons' full-sized donuts, fried in shortening, and coated in flavor-specific glazes, sugars, or fillings. Whether you're grabbing a quick snack on your way to work or ordering a big box for a party, Timbits are always a crowd-pleaser.
